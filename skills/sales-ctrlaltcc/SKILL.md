## Step 3 — CtrlAlt.cc platform reference

### How CtrlAlt.cc works

CtrlAlt.cc is a curated directory of startup tools focused on lesser-known products that "fly under the radar." Unlike Product Hunt's 24-hour launch window, CtrlAlt.cc is a discovery directory where products are featured daily and organized across 26 categories.

**Key difference**: Curated by a single person (Daniela, UX designer) — the directory has an editorial voice and manual curation. Products are reviewed before listing.

#### Submission flow
1. Create an account on CtrlAlt.cc
2. Submit your product: URL, short description (max 1 paragraph), launch date
3. Check "I am the maker" for maker verification
4. **Free submission**: enters editorial review queue (up to 2 months wait)
5. **Paid launch ($39)**: skip queue, guaranteed homepage feature for 1 week

#### Visibility system

|---|---|---|
| Daily featured | Curated products shown on homepage daily | Homepage visibility on your launch date |
| Categories | 26 categories (AI, Marketing, Developer tools, etc.) | Discovery by category browsing |
| Upvoting | Community votes on products | Higher visibility within daily listings |
| Streaks | Consecutive-day engagement tracking (66-day milestone) | Gamified community participation |
| Newsletter | "Product of the Week" — directory updated every Friday | Email distribution to subscribers |

#### Categories (26)

### Free vs paid decision

**Pay $39 when:**
- You have a specific launch date or campaign window
- You need the dofollow backlink soon (for an SEO sprint)
- You want guaranteed homepage feature for a full week
- You're coordinating a multi-platform launch and need timing control

**Stay free when:**
- Budget is zero and timing is flexible
- You're submitting to many directories and this is a low-priority one
- You just want the backlink eventually and don't need visibility

### Multi-directory launch strategy

CtrlAlt.cc works best as one platform in a broader launch. Use `/sales-launch-directory` for the full cross-platform strategy, but here's a quick sequence:

1. **SoloPush** — instant listing, no queue (DR46-47 backlink)

## Gotchas

*Best-effort from research — review these, especially items about plan-gated features and integration gotchas that may be outdated.*

- **Small, solo-run platform.** CtrlAlt.cc is curated by one person (Daniela). Response times and review queue depend on her availability. Don't expect the same throughput as larger platforms.
- **2-month free queue is real.** Multiple sources confirm the wait. If you need timely listing, the $39 paid option is the only way to guarantee it.
- **DA30 is modest.** Significantly lower than other directories in this category (ProductBurst DR46, MicroLaunch DR59, SideProjectors DR69). The backlink has value but is on the lower end.
- **Traffic numbers conflict.** LaunchPedia reports <5K/mo, other sources say 15.7K. Actual traffic is likely on the lower end. Don't expect significant visitor volume.
- **No API or programmatic access.** Everything is manual — submission, updates, engagement. No way to automate.
- **WordPress/Elementor stack.** The platform is functional but not as polished or feature-rich as purpose-built launch platforms. Limited analytics for makers.
- **Newsletter reach unknown.** "Product of the Week" exists but subscriber count is not documented. The email distribution value is unverified.
- **No support email.** Only contact channels are X (@nerddoingsocial) and Instagram (@ctrlaltcc). Response times may vary.

## Troubleshooting

### Listing stuck in review queue
**Symptom**: Submitted weeks ago and product hasn't appeared on the site
**Cause**: Free submissions enter an editorial queue managed by a single curator. Wait times of up to 2 months are documented.
**Solution**: If timing matters, upgrade to the $39 paid launch to skip the queue. Otherwise, be patient — the listing will eventually be reviewed. Use the wait time to submit to instant-listing platforms like ProductBurst and SoloPush for immediate visibility.

### Not getting engagement after listing
**Symptom**: Product is listed but barely getting upvotes or attention
**Cause**: CtrlAlt.cc has modest traffic (~5K-15.7K/mo). Organic discovery alone may not drive significant engagement, especially outside the featured week.
**Solution**: Share your CtrlAlt.cc listing on Twitter, relevant subreddits, and communities. Engage with the platform's streak system to increase your profile visibility. Don't rely on CtrlAlt.cc as a primary traffic source — use it as one part of a multi-directory launch strategy.

### Unsure if the backlink is actually dofollow
**Symptom**: Want to verify the backlink type before investing time
**Cause**: Third-party sources report dofollow, but individual listings may vary.
**Solution**: After listing, inspect the link. Right-click your product link on the CtrlAlt.cc page → Inspect Element → check for `rel="nofollow"`. If absent, the link is dofollow. If present, the listing still has brand and referral value. The $39 paid launch explicitly mentions dofollow links.
