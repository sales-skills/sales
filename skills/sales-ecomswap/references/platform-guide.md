# Ecomswap Platform Reference

## Platform overview

Ecomswap is a boutique sell-side M&A advisory firm specialized in profitable ecommerce and digital-asset exits, founded by Eliott Bucher and Jad Kiwan. Positioning (from their homepage): **"Expert M&A for eCommerce Founders powered by a global network of verified buyers."** They explicitly frame themselves as filling the gap between traditional investment banks (too expensive and enterprise-focused for sub-$10M deals) and open marketplaces (low-quality buyers, no hand-holding).

- **Founded**: Ecomswap brand active 2023+, operated by serial ecommerce founders
- **Geography**: US, Europe, UAE presence
- **Deals closed**: 100+
- **Buyer network**: 50K+ pre-qualified investors, $2B+ aggregate verified capital
- **Advertised close rate**: 93% (on deals that reach LOI)
- **Advertised time-to-exit**: <2 months average from engagement

Target audience: profitable founder-operated Shopify, Amazon FBA, DTC ecommerce, SaaS, newsletter, and content-site owners looking for a curated, confidential sale process with advisory support.

## Valuation methodology

### SDE-based profit multiples

Ecomswap uses **Seller's Discretionary Earnings (SDE)** as the primary valuation base:

> **SDE = Net Income + Owner's Salary + Interest + Taxes + Depreciation + Discretionary Addbacks**

Discretionary addbacks include: one-time expenses, owner-only perks (travel, vehicle, phone), non-essential professional fees, family members on payroll.

### Typical multiples for Ecomswap's asset mix

| Asset type | Typical SDE multiple | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Shopify / DTC ecom | 2.5–3.5x SDE | Lower than SaaS; product-dependent |
| Amazon FBA | 2.5–3.5x SDE | Platform risk discounts multiple |
| Newsletter / content | 2.5–4x SDE | Audience stickiness drives premium |
| SaaS | 3–5x SDE | Higher multiple for recurring revenue |
| Monetized YouTube | 2–3x SDE | Platform dependency risk |

### Premium drivers

- Clean 24+ months of verified financials (Stripe, Shopify exports)
- Diversified revenue (no customer/channel >30%)
- Recurring revenue component
- Documented SOPs and transferable operations
- Proprietary brand/IP vs dropship/reseller

### Discount drivers

- Founder-dependent (personal brand, celebrity voice)
- Platform-dependent (single Shopify app, single ad platform >60% of traffic)
- Declining revenue trend
- Concentrated customer/supplier base
- Unverifiable financials

## Workflow and process

### 1. Free confidential valuation

- Request via contact form on ecomswap.io
- Submit basic financials (top-line revenue, net profit, SDE, asset type, platform stack)
- Ecomswap M&A team reviews and schedules a call
- Call covers valuation range, realistic multiples, comparable deals
- **No cost, no obligation** — this is the lead-generation funnel

### 2. Engagement letter (if proceeding)

- Terms negotiated privately — not disclosed publicly
- Typical boutique brokerage terms: 6–12 month exclusivity, success fee on close, possible retainer
- Get this in writing and compare against Empire Flippers / FE International before signing

### 3. Pre-listing preparation

- Data room assembly: P&L (24 months), Stripe/Shopify/Amazon exports, analytics access, SOPs, customer/supplier concentration
- Confidential Information Memorandum (CIM) drafted by Ecomswap team
- No public listing — buyers are sourced privately

### 4. Buyer sourcing

- Ecomswap presents the opportunity to shortlisted buyers from their 50K+ network
- Buyers sign NDAs before seeing CIM
- Advertised 30-day window to competitive offers
- Buyers are pre-qualified for proof-of-funds

### 5. LOI negotiation

- Buyer submits Letter of Intent (non-binding except exclusivity)
- Key terms: price, structure (asset vs share), earnout, exclusivity, transition support
- Ecomswap advises; seller decides

### 6. Due diligence (post-LOI)

- Granular data room access under exclusivity
- Technical audit, customer interviews (with consent), financial deep-dive
- Typical DD duration: 30–60 days

### 7. APA and close

- Asset Purchase Agreement negotiated (or Share Purchase, if tax-driven)
- Escrow opened (Ecomswap references "secure escrow" — specific provider not named publicly, likely Escrow.com)
- Assets transfer, funds release
- Typical LOI-to-close: 30–60 days
- Advertised total time from engagement: <2 months average

## Deep dives

### SDE vs EBITDA — why it matters

For founder-operated businesses, **SDE is always larger than EBITDA** because SDE adds back the owner's salary and discretionary expenses. A business with $200K EBITDA and a $150K owner salary has $350K SDE.

Multiples applied to SDE are therefore lower than multiples applied to EBITDA — "3x on SDE" and "3x on EBITDA" describe very different valuations for the same business.

Ecomswap uses SDE because:
- Most sellers are founder-operators (SDE-friendly)
- Matches Main Street M&A and micro-acquisition norms
- Allows apples-to-apples comparison with Empire Flippers, Quiet Light, and Acquire.com

FE International and larger PE-focused brokers use EBITDA because buyers are often strategic or institutional (no SDE-style addbacks).

### Red flags to check before signing engagement

- **Written engagement letter with full fee schedule** — not verbal "we'll work something out"
- **Exclusivity period under 9 months** — longer parks your business
- **Clear carve-out for buyers you've already spoken to** — prevents paying commission on pre-existing leads
- **Defined termination clauses** — what happens if no offers in 60/90 days
- **References from 3+ closed deals** — reputable brokers provide these
- **Clarity on who handles legal/accounting** — and who pays for it
