## Step 3 — Employee advocacy strategy reference

### Why employee advocacy works

- Employee posts get **8x more engagement** than brand posts on average
- Employee networks are **10x larger** than a company's follower base combined
- **64% of buyers** cite shared values as the primary reason for a brand relationship — employee voices build trust faster than brand accounts
- **Costs 1/10th** of paid social for equivalent reach

#### When to choose each platform

- **Sprout Social Advocacy** — best if you already use Sprout for social media management and want advocacy in the same platform. Marketing curates content once, employees share from the same tool. Limitation: requires Sprout subscription on top of advocacy add-on.
- **Hootsuite Amplify** — best if you already use Hootsuite. Similar rationale — one platform for management + advocacy. Less AI-powered than dedicated tools.
- **DSMN8** — best for mid-market companies wanting AI-powered personalization. Generates unique post variations for each employee so posts don't look identical. Trusted by McKinsey, Nokia, Dropbox.
- **GaggleAMP** — best for B2B companies that want structured "activities" (specific asks like "share this post" or "comment on this thread"). Manager assigns activities, employees complete them. Stronger gamification.
- **EveryoneSocial** — best for enterprise scale. Designed for large workforces with AI-powered content suggestions and robust analytics.
- **Sociabble** — best for global enterprises that need employee advocacy combined with internal communications in one platform. Unique in combining external sharing with internal content distribution.

### Designing an advocacy program

#### Phase 1 — Foundation (weeks 1-2)

1. **Define goals and KPIs**
   - Awareness: earned reach, impressions, share of voice
   - Leads: link clicks, form fills from employee posts
   - Recruiting: application referrals, employer brand sentiment
   - Set a specific target: "Increase brand reach by 40% in Q3"

2. **Identify pilot group**
   - Start with 20-50 enthusiastic employees (not the whole company)
   - Mix departments: sales, marketing, HR, product, leadership
   - Choose people who already post on LinkedIn — they'll adopt fastest
   - Executive participation is critical — it signals legitimacy

3. **Create content guidelines**
   - What employees CAN share: company news, industry insights, team wins, culture content
   - What employees should NOT share: unannounced products, financial data, customer names (without permission), political content
   - Voice: employees share in their own voice, not corporate speak. Authenticity > polish.
   - Disclaimers: whether to add "opinions my own" (check legal requirements)

4. **Set up the tool**
   - Configure content library with 20-30 pieces of shareable content
   - Enable SSO for frictionless onboarding
   - Set up gamification (points per share, weekly leaderboard)
   - Create 3-5 content categories (industry news, company updates, culture, thought leadership)

#### Phase 2 — Launch (weeks 3-4)

1. **Onboard the pilot group**
   - 30-minute kickoff session: why advocacy matters, how to use the tool, what's in it for them
   - Show the tool — make the first share happen in the session
   - Set expectations: 1-2 shares per week is plenty. No pressure.

2. **Seed the content library**
   - Pre-load 2-4 weeks of shareable content
   - Mix formats: articles, images, short posts, company announcements
   - Write 2-3 suggested caption variations per piece (employees can edit)

3. **Drive early momentum**
   - Celebrate first shares publicly (Slack shout-outs, email highlights)
   - Share reach metrics weekly ("our team's posts reached 50,000 people this week")
   - Run a launch contest (most shares in week 1 wins a prize)

#### Phase 3 — Scale (month 2+)

1. **Expand to more employees** — invite new cohorts monthly, don't blast the whole company at once
2. **Diversify content** — add employee-generated content, not just marketing-curated
3. **Integrate into existing workflows** — Slack notifications, email digests, mobile push
4. **Connect to business outcomes** — track clicks to website, form fills, sales conversations started from employee shares

### Driving participation

The biggest challenge in employee advocacy is sustaining participation. Here's what works:

| Tactic | Impact | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| **Executive participation** | Very high — signals importance | Low — they just need to share too |
| **Gamification** | High — competitions drive short-term spikes | Low — most tools have this built in |
| **Content quality** | High — employees won't share boring content | Medium — requires ongoing curation |
| **Make it easy** | High — one-click sharing from mobile | Low — tool setup |
| **Personal benefit framing** | High — "build your personal brand" resonates more than "help the company" | Low — messaging change |
| **Remove friction** | Medium — pre-written captions, mobile app, push notifications | Low — configuration |
| **Recognition** | Medium — public shout-outs for top advocates | Low — weekly Slack message |
| **Mandatory participation** | Negative — kills authenticity and causes resentment | N/A — don't do this |

### Compliance and brand safety

- **Pre-approved content only** — employees share from a curated library, not random company content
- **Editable captions** — employees can personalize (authenticity) but start from an approved base
- **Regulated industries** (finance, healthcare, pharma) — extra requirements:
  - All content must be compliance-reviewed before entering the library
  - Employee posts may need archiving for regulatory audit trails
  - Some platforms (GaggleAMP, Sociabble) support compliance archiving
- **GDPR considerations** — advocacy programs collect employee social data; ensure privacy policy covers this
- **Off-boarding** — when an employee leaves, their advocacy access should be revoked immediately

### Measuring advocacy ROI

| Metric | What it measures | How to calculate |
|---|---|---|
| **Earned media value (EMV)** | Equivalent ad spend for organic reach | Total reach × industry CPM |
| **Program adoption** | % of invited employees actively sharing | Active sharers ÷ total enrolled |
| **Share rate** | Content engagement by employees | Posts shared ÷ content available |
| **Click-through rate** | Traffic from employee posts | Clicks from advocacy links ÷ total shares |
| **Pipeline influenced** | Deals where advocacy touchpoint exists | CRM attribution from UTM-tagged links |
| **Cost per reach** | Efficiency vs. paid social | Tool cost ÷ total advocacy reach |

**Healthy benchmarks:**
- Adoption rate: 30-50% of enrolled employees actively sharing
- Share rate: 2-3 shares per employee per week
- EMV multiple: 3-5x the cost of the advocacy tool
- Click-through: 2-5% from employee posts (higher than brand posts)

## Gotchas

*Best-effort from research — review these, especially items about platform capabilities and pricing that may change.*

- **Mandatory participation backfires.** Forcing employees to share makes content feel inauthentic and causes resentment. The best programs make sharing easy and rewarding, not required.
- **Content quality is the #1 participation driver.** If employees wouldn't share the content on their own, gamification won't fix it. Invest in creating genuinely shareable content — industry insights, team wins, and thought leadership outperform press releases.
- **Executive participation is non-negotiable.** If the CEO and VPs aren't sharing, employees won't either. Get leadership to commit before launching.
- **Don't expect instant scale.** Start with 20-50 enthusiastic employees, prove value, then expand. Company-wide launches have high initial sign-ups and low sustained engagement.
- **Advocacy tools add cost on top of social management.** Sprout Advocacy requires a Sprout subscription + advocacy add-on. DSMN8 is $5-8/user/month. For a 200-person program on DSMN8, that's $1,000-1,600/month. Budget accordingly.
- **Employees sharing identical content looks bad.** If 20 people post the same LinkedIn post with the same caption, it's obviously corporate. Use tools that generate personalized variations (DSMN8) or strongly encourage employees to edit captions.

## Troubleshooting

### Low employee participation after launch
**Symptom**: Initial sign-ups were strong but weekly sharing has dropped to under 20%
**Cause**: Content fatigue, no ongoing motivation, tool friction, or lack of visible leadership participation
**Solution**: Refresh the content library with new formats (short posts, images, polls). Launch a 2-week competition with meaningful prizes. Get the CEO to post and share the leaderboard. Reduce friction with push notifications and one-click sharing from mobile. Reframe the value: "build your LinkedIn presence" resonates more than "help marketing".

### Content looks robotic and corporate
**Symptom**: Employee posts all look identical, and connections are calling it out
**Cause**: Employees sharing pre-written captions without personalization
**Solution**: Switch to tools that generate unique caption variations per employee (DSMN8). Coach employees to add one personal sentence to every share. Remove the most corporate-sounding content from the library. Feature examples of good personalized shares.

### Can't prove ROI to leadership
**Symptom**: Program is running but leadership questions the investment
**Cause**: Not tracking the right metrics or connecting to business outcomes
**Solution**: Set up UTM tracking on all advocacy links. Calculate EMV monthly (total reach × industry CPM). Track clicks-to-website and compare to paid social cost-per-click. For B2B, track which deals had an advocacy touchpoint in the buyer journey via CRM attribution.
