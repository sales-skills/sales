# Gaming Influencer Marketing Platform Guide

## How gaming influencer marketing differs from general influencer marketing

Gaming has unique dynamics that general influencer guides miss:

| Factor | General influencer marketing | Gaming influencer marketing |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary metric** | Sales, engagement | Wishlists, installs, concurrent players |
| **Content format** | Posts, reels, stories | Let's Play, livestreams, first impressions, trailers |
| **Creator relationship** | Transactional (post and done) | Ongoing (early access, updates, DLC coverage) |
| **Compensation** | Cash + product | Cash + game keys + early access + revenue share |
| **Platform priority** | Instagram, TikTok | YouTube, Twitch, TikTok |
| **Audience intent** | Browse and buy | Watch, wishlist, then buy during sales |
| **Launch timing** | Evergreen | Concentrated around launch window |

## Finding gaming influencers

### Where to search

| Source | Best for | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Cloutboost Portal** | Largest gaming-specific database (1.5M+ creators) with game-genre filters | Use advanced filters: genre, specific game titles, platform, follower range |
| **YouTube search** | Finding creators who covered similar games | Search "[similar game] let's play" or "[similar game] review", filter by view count |
| **Twitch directories** | Finding active streamers in your genre | Browse game category directories, check SullyGnome or TwitchTracker for analytics |
| **TikTok search** | Short-form gaming content creators | Search gaming hashtags (#indiegame, #[genre]game, #gaming) |
| **General platforms** | Cross-referencing gaming creators with audience data | influData (covers Twitch), Heepsy (covers Twitch), Modash, CreatorIQ |
| **Game key platforms** | Creators who actively seek games to cover | Keymailer (55K opted-in gaming creators), Lurkit, Woovit |

### Creator tiers for gaming

| Tier | Followers | Typical cost | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nano** (1K-10K) | Small but dedicated | Game key only | Organic buzz, Steam reviews, community building |
| **Micro** (10K-100K) | Engaged niche audience | $200-1,500 + key | Wishlist generation, genre communities |
| **Mid** (100K-500K) | Established voice in genre | $1,500-5,000 + key | Launch week visibility, sustained coverage |
| **Macro** (500K-1M) | Broad gaming audience | $5,000-15,000 + key | Mass awareness, trailer features |
| **Mega** (1M+) | Mainstream gaming presence | $15,000-50,000+ | AAA campaigns, cultural moments |

*Gaming rates tend to be 20-40% lower than general lifestyle influencer rates because many gaming creators genuinely want to play new games and accept keys or reduced rates.*

### Vetting gaming creators

Beyond the standard vetting framework (see `/sales-influencer-marketing`), gaming creators need additional checks:

1. **Genre alignment** — do they regularly play your genre, or is this a one-off?
2. **Completion rate** — do they finish games or abandon after one session? Incomplete coverage is less valuable.
3. **Audience platform** — if you're launching on Steam, prioritize creators whose audience plays on PC
4. **Content tone** — does their style match your game's tone? A horror game creator playing your cozy farming sim won't resonate.
5. **Review history** — are they honest reviewers or paid promoters? Authentic creators drive better conversion.

## Game launch campaign sequencing

### Pre-launch (4-8 weeks before launch)

| Week | Action | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **-8 to -6** | Send game keys to nano/micro creators (no-strings gifting) | Organic buzz, early Steam reviews |
| **-6 to -4** | Secure mid-tier creators for embargo'd first-impression videos | Wishlist spike timed to trailer drop |
| **-4 to -2** | Launch demo + send to all tiers | Demo coverage drives wishlists |
| **-2 to -1** | Embargo'd review copies to priority creators | Review videos ready for launch day |
| **Launch day** | Coordinated publish of all embargo'd content | Maximum launch-day visibility |
| **+1 to +2** | Livestream partnerships (Twitch drops, sponsored streams) | Sustain player count, drive sales tail |
| **+2 to +4** | Performance review + re-engage top performers for ongoing coverage | DLC teasers, update coverage |

### Content format strategy

| Format | Platform | Cost | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **First impression / Let's Play** | YouTube | $$$ | Wishlist generation (viewers see gameplay) |
| **Sponsored livestream** | Twitch | $$ | Launch day/week visibility, concurrent player spike |
| **Short-form clip** | TikTok, YouTube Shorts | $ | Viral reach, younger audience, top-of-funnel |
| **Review / critique** | YouTube | $$$$ | Credibility, long-tail discovery |
| **Gameplay trailer feature** | YouTube | $$ | Announcement amplification |
| **Twitch Drops integration** | Twitch | Setup cost only | Player engagement, sustained viewership |

## Budget allocation

### By game stage

| Stage | Budget split |
|---|---|
| **Wishlist phase** | 60% YouTube (Let's Play/first impressions), 30% TikTok (short-form clips), 10% Twitch (early streams) |
| **Launch week** | 40% YouTube (reviews, Let's Play), 40% Twitch (sponsored streams, Drops), 20% TikTok |
| **Post-launch** | 50% Twitch (ongoing streams, events), 30% YouTube (update coverage), 20% TikTok |

### By budget level

| Budget | Recommended approach |
|---|---|
| **Keys only ($0)** | Send to 50-100 nano creators via Keymailer/Woovit. Focus on creators who cover your genre. Expect 10-20% coverage rate. |
| **$1K-5K** | 5-10 micro creators (paid) + 30-50 nano (keys only). Mix of YouTube Let's Play + TikTok clips. |
| **$5K-25K** | 2-3 mid-tier + 10-15 micro + 50+ nano. Consider Cloutboost managed service ($5K min). Full launch sequence. |
| **$25K-100K** | 1-2 macro + 5-10 mid + 20-30 micro + 100+ nano. Agency recommended. Multi-platform coordinated campaign. |
| **$100K+** | Macro/mega partnerships + full agency support. Cloutboost or similar gaming-specific agency. Retargeting from influencer content. |

## Measuring gaming influencer ROI

### Gaming-specific metrics

| Metric | How to measure | Good benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| **Wishlists per video** | Steam UTM links or post-video wishlist spike | 500-2,000 per mid-tier creator video |
| **Cost per wishlist** | Total spend ÷ attributed wishlists | $0.50-3.00 (micro creators closer to $0.50) |
| **Install conversion** | Wishlists → purchases on launch day | 15-25% wishlist-to-purchase conversion |
| **Concurrent player spike** | SteamDB or platform analytics during/after stream | Correlate with stream timing |
| **Steam traffic source** | Steam Marketing → UTM tracking | Track creator-specific referral links |
| **Content longevity** | Video views at 30/60/90 days | Evergreen Let's Play > sponsored stream (short shelf life) |
| **Demo downloads** | Platform analytics during demo campaign | Track per-creator attribution |

### Attribution for gaming

- **Steam UTM links** — Steam supports custom campaign tracking URLs. Give each creator a unique URL.
- **Promo codes** — if selling direct (Epic, itch.io), use unique codes per creator
- **Wishlist surge timing** — correlate wishlist spikes with video publish times (imprecise but directional)
- **Post-purchase surveys** — "How did you hear about this game?" in-game or on Discord
- **Steam Playtest / Demo tracking** — track demo sign-ups attributed to specific creators
