# Help Desk Platform Comparison

## Zendesk
- **Best for**: Mid-market to enterprise, complex multi-brand support
- **Pricing**: $19-$169/agent/mo (add-ons can double this)
- **Strengths**: Deepest feature set, 1,300+ marketplace apps, strongest Salesforce integration, comprehensive API (15+ API families), mature ecosystem
- **Weaknesses**: Expensive with add-ons (AI/Copilot/QA/WFM each ~$25-50 extra), complex setup, slow customer support, steep learning curve
- **AI**: Advanced AI Agents and Copilot available as paid add-ons (~$50/agent each). Generative replies included in Suite Team+
- **Channels**: Email, chat, phone, social, WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram, SMS
- **Best fit**: Teams needing enterprise-grade customization, multi-brand support (up to 300 help centers), deep integrations, and extensive automation

## Freshdesk (Freshworks)
- **Best for**: Budget-conscious teams, SMB to mid-market
- **Pricing**: Free (2 agents) / Growth $15 / Pro $49 / Enterprise $79 per agent/mo
- **Strengths**: Free tier, intuitive UI, fast setup (days not weeks), Freddy AI included on paid plans, strong automation
- **Weaknesses**: Less customizable than Zendesk at enterprise scale, marketplace smaller (~1000 apps), reporting less powerful on lower tiers
- **AI**: Freddy AI auto-solves queries in 20+ languages, included on paid plans (not an add-on)
- **Channels**: Email, chat, phone, social, WhatsApp
- **Best fit**: Teams wanting Zendesk-level features at lower cost, especially those starting with a free plan and scaling up

## Intercom
- **Best for**: SaaS companies, product-led growth, conversational support
- **Pricing**: Essential $29/seat + $0.99/resolution / Advanced $85/seat / Expert $132/seat
- **Strengths**: Best messenger UX, proactive messaging, product tours, in-app support, sophisticated chatbot (Fin AI), beautiful design
- **Weaknesses**: Conversation-based pricing makes costs variable, less suited for traditional ticket-heavy support, expensive at high volume
- **AI**: Fin AI agent resolves up to 50% of queries, included in pricing (conversation fees apply)
- **Channels**: Messenger widget, email, WhatsApp, Facebook, Instagram, SMS, in-app
- **Best fit**: Product-led SaaS companies wanting conversational support with proactive messaging and in-app engagement

## Help Scout
- **Best for**: Small to mid-size teams wanting simplicity
- **Pricing**: Free (up to 50 contacts) / Standard $22 / Plus $44 / Pro $65 per user/mo
- **Strengths**: Clean shared inbox feel, fast setup, Docs knowledge base, customer profiles, Beacon widget
- **Weaknesses**: No phone channel built in, limited automation compared to Zendesk, smaller marketplace
- **AI**: AI assist for drafts and summaries, AI answers from knowledge base
- **Channels**: Email, chat (Beacon widget), social
- **Best fit**: Teams that want email-like simplicity with just enough features, strong knowledge base, and fast time-to-value

## Zoho Desk
- **Best for**: Budget teams in the Zoho ecosystem
- **Pricing**: Free (3 agents) / Express $7 / Standard $14 / Professional $23 / Enterprise $40 per agent/mo
- **Strengths**: Cheapest full-featured option, deep Zoho suite integration (CRM, Projects, Analytics), Zia AI assistant, customer portal
- **Weaknesses**: UI less polished, best value only if already on Zoho, limited marketplace outside Zoho ecosystem
- **AI**: Zia AI for sentiment analysis, ticket classification, response suggestions
- **Channels**: Email, chat, phone, social, WhatsApp
- **Best fit**: Teams already on Zoho CRM/suite, or anyone needing full omnichannel support on a tight budget

## Front
- **Best for**: Teams wanting shared inbox with collaboration
- **Pricing**: Starter $19 / Growth $59 / Scale $99 / Premier $229 per seat/mo
- **Strengths**: Combines personal + shared email, strong internal collaboration (comments, assignments), CRM-like contact management, modern UI
- **Weaknesses**: No built-in phone/voice, less structured than traditional ticketing, expensive at scale
- **AI**: AI tagging, AI drafts, chatbot resolution
- **Channels**: Email, SMS, chat, social, WhatsApp
- **Best fit**: Teams that live in email and want collaborative inbox with CRM context, not traditional ticketing

## Hiver
- **Best for**: Gmail-native teams
- **Pricing**: Free (unlimited users) / Lite $19 / Growth $29 / Pro $49 per user/mo
- **Strengths**: Works inside Gmail (no new tool to learn), free tier, shared mailbox, AI-powered (Harvey AI), fast setup
- **Weaknesses**: Gmail-dependent (no standalone option), less scalable for large teams, limited channel coverage
- **AI**: Harvey AI for drafting, summarizing, and automating email responses
- **Channels**: Email (Gmail), chat, WhatsApp, voice
- **Best fit**: Small teams already on Google Workspace who want help desk inside Gmail

## Jira Service Management
- **Best for**: Dev/IT teams, ITSM
- **Pricing**: Free (3 agents) / Standard $18.75 / Premium $46.25 / Enterprise custom per agent/mo
- **Strengths**: Deep Jira Software integration, ITIL-aligned (incident, problem, change management), Confluence knowledge base, CI/CD visibility
- **Weaknesses**: Complex for non-technical users, less suited for customer-facing support, UI designed for IT not end-customers
- **AI**: Atlassian Intelligence for ticket summarization, virtual agent
- **Channels**: Email, customer portal, Slack, Teams
- **Best fit**: Engineering and IT teams needing tight alignment between support tickets and development issues

## Gorgias
- **Best for**: E-commerce customer service
- **Pricing**: Starter $10/50 tickets / Basic $40/300 / Pro $360/2000 / Advanced $900/5000 per month
- **Strengths**: Deep Shopify/BigCommerce/Magento integration, order management inside tickets (refunds, cancellations, tracking), revenue-focused metrics, per-ticket pricing
- **Weaknesses**: Only for e-commerce (not general purpose), expensive at high ticket volume, limited ITSM/IT use case
- **AI**: AI agent resolves common e-commerce queries (order status, refund policy)
- **Channels**: Email, chat, social, SMS, phone, WhatsApp
- **Best fit**: Shopify/BigCommerce stores wanting deep e-commerce integration with order management in support tickets

## Tidio
- **Best for**: SMBs wanting AI chatbot + live chat
- **Pricing**: Free / Starter $29 / Growth $59 / Plus $749 / Premium $2999 per month (not per agent)
- **Strengths**: Easy chatbot builder, AI chatbot (Lyro), live chat, affordable for small teams, Shopify/WordPress integration
- **Weaknesses**: Limited at enterprise scale, per-conversation pricing on AI features, less suited for complex ticket management
- **AI**: Lyro AI chatbot with task automation, learns from knowledge base
- **Channels**: Chat, email, Messenger, Instagram
- **Best fit**: Small businesses wanting an AI chatbot that handles common questions with live chat fallback

## Crisp
- **Best for**: Startups wanting affordable multi-channel
- **Pricing**: Free (2 seats) / Pro $25 / Unlimited $95 per month per workspace (not per agent)
- **Strengths**: Per-workspace pricing (unlimited agents on higher tiers), live chat + chatbot, shared inbox, knowledge base, simple UI
- **Weaknesses**: Smaller marketplace, less enterprise features, limited phone integration
- **AI**: AI chatbot, AI assist for agents
- **Channels**: Chat, email, Messenger, Instagram, WhatsApp, phone (via integration)
- **Best fit**: Startups and small teams wanting per-workspace pricing instead of per-agent

## LiveAgent
- **Best for**: Budget omnichannel with built-in phone
- **Pricing**: Free / Small $9 / Medium $29 / Large $49 / Enterprise $69 per agent/mo
- **Strengths**: Built-in call center (no add-on needed), 130+ ticketing features, gamification, affordable
- **Weaknesses**: UI feels dated, less AI sophistication, smaller marketplace
- **AI**: Basic AI answer assistant
- **Channels**: Email, chat, phone (built-in), social, forum
- **Best fit**: Teams needing phone support included in base price without separate CCaaS

## Kustomer
- **Best for**: Customer-centric timeline view
- **Pricing**: Enterprise $89 / Ultimate $139 per user/mo
- **Strengths**: Customer timeline (all interactions in one view), AI-powered automation, CRM-like customer profiles
- **Weaknesses**: Expensive, smaller marketplace, now owned by Meta (acquisition concerns)
- **AI**: AI agent (KIQ), intent detection, sentiment analysis
- **Channels**: Email, chat, social, SMS, WhatsApp, phone
- **Best fit**: Support-heavy companies wanting a customer-centric view rather than ticket-centric

## Pylon
- **Best for**: B2B support via Slack/Teams
- **Pricing**: Custom pricing
- **Strengths**: Native Slack, Teams, Discord support channel management, account-level intelligence, B2B-focused
- **Weaknesses**: Newer platform (less mature), limited traditional ticketing features, custom pricing only
- **AI**: AI workflows, automated triage
- **Channels**: Slack, Teams, Discord, email, in-app
- **Best fit**: B2B companies whose customers expect support in Slack or Teams channels

## HappyFox
- **Best for**: Mid-market with strong automation
- **Pricing**: Basic $14 / Team $69 / Pro $119 / Enterprise PRO custom per agent/mo
- **Strengths**: Clean task management, strong SLA management, multi-brand, good reporting
- **Weaknesses**: Less known brand, smaller community, phone/chat as add-ons
- **AI**: AI assist, chatbot
- **Channels**: Email, chat, phone, social
- **Best fit**: Mid-market teams wanting structured task management with clean SLA tracking
