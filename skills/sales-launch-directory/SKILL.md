# Startup Directory Launch Strategy

Helps the user plan and execute a coordinated launch across multiple startup directories and discovery platforms — choosing which to submit to, in what order, and how to optimize each listing for maximum visibility, backlinks, and early users.

## Step 1 — Gather context

Ask the user:

1. **What are you launching?**
   - A) SaaS / software tool
   - B) Mobile app
   - C) API / developer tool
   - D) No-code / low-code tool
   - E) Physical product or service
   - F) Something else — describe it

2. **What stage is your product?**
   - A) Idea / concept — no working product yet
   - B) MVP / prototype — functional but early
   - C) Launched — live with some users
   - D) Growing — looking for more distribution

3. **What's your primary goal?**
   - A) First users and feedback
   - B) SEO backlinks
   - C) Community visibility and social proof
   - D) All of the above

4. **What's your budget for paid listings?**
   - A) $0 — free submissions only
   - B) Under $50 — can afford one or two paid options
   - C) $100-500 — willing to invest in expedited/featured placements
   - D) Budget is flexible — optimize for results

5. **Any timing constraints?**
   - A) ASAP — want listings live as fast as possible
   - B) Coordinating with a specific launch date
   - C) No rush — steady rollout over weeks/months

**If the user's request already provides most of this context, skip directly to the relevant step.** Lead with your best-effort answer using reasonable assumptions (stated explicitly), then ask only the most critical 1-2 clarifying questions at the end.

## Step 4 — Actionable guidance

### Launch sequencing strategy

The key principle: **submit to instant-listing platforms first, editorial-review platforms second, and save Product Hunt for last** (when your listing is refined from feedback).

#### Phase 1 — Instant listings (Day 1-3)
Submit to platforms with no editorial queue to get live immediately:
1. **SoloPush** — instant listing, no gatekeeping, solo maker community
2. **ProductBurst** — instant listing, daily/weekly ranking opportunities
3. **Super Launch** — instant listing, 7-day guaranteed front page (note: free backlink is nofollow)
3. **DevHunt** — instant listing, free dofollow backlink (DR57-62), developer tools only, requires GitHub auth
4. **JustGotFound** — instant listing, free dofollow backlink (DR23 — low but still free)
4. **Huzzler Launch Arena** — submit Monday for weekly competition (Wed-Sun voting)
4. **Peerlist Launchpad** — submit for Monday launch, all projects featured, streak-based vote multipliers (nofollow backlink, DR76 — community value, not SEO)
4. **Uneed (Skip the Line, $29.99)** — choose your launch date, DR74 dofollow backlink (one of the highest free directory backlinks)
4. **Indie Hackers** — post in relevant groups, share your story (nofollow backlink, DR75 — community value, not SEO)
4. **Altern** — submit if your product is AI-focused (dofollow backlink, editorial review queue)

**Goal**: Get live listings, initial backlinks, and early feedback to refine your messaging.

### Tracking and measuring results

For each directory submission, track:
- **UTM parameters**: Add `?utm_source={platform}&utm_medium=directory&utm_campaign=launch` to your URL
- **Backlink verification**: Use Ahrefs, Moz, or Google Search Console to confirm dofollow backlinks appear
- **Traffic**: Monitor referral traffic from each directory in Google Analytics
- **Conversions**: Track signups/purchases by referral source

### Common mistakes

1. **Launching on Product Hunt first.** PH is highest-stakes — use smaller directories to refine your listing first.
2. **Copy-pasting the same description everywhere.** Tailor your listing to each platform's audience and format.
3. **Submitting and forgetting.** Directories with community features (upvoting, comments) reward engagement. Check back and respond.
4. **Ignoring free editorial queues.** Submit to all free queues on Day 1 even if you're also paying for fast-track. The free listings provide long-tail value months later.
5. **Overweighting backlink DR.** A DR46 backlink from a relevant directory is still valuable. Don't skip lower-DR directories — cumulative effect matters.

## Gotchas

*Best-effort from research — review these, especially items about plan-gated features and integration gotchas that may be outdated.*

- **Pricing changes.** All prices listed are best-effort snapshots. MicroLaunch Pro, LaunchingNext expedited, CtrlAlt.cc paid launch, and BetaList pricing may change. Verify before purchasing.
- **DR/DA numbers fluctuate.** Domain ratings change over time. The rankings above are directionally correct but exact numbers may shift.
- **Product Hunt is not a directory.** It's a launch event. Don't treat it like the others — it requires separate preparation, community building, and timing strategy.
- **"Dofollow" claims vary.** Some directories report dofollow but individual listings may vary. Always verify after your listing goes live by inspecting the HTML.
- **Editorial queues are unpredictable.** "Up to 2 months" is a documented worst case, not a guarantee. Some platforms review faster, some slower.
- **Small platforms have small traffic.** Most directories in this list have modest traffic (5K-25K/mo). Set realistic expectations — directory submissions are a long-term SEO play, not an overnight traffic source.

## Troubleshooting

### Submitted to many directories but getting no traffic
**Symptom**: Listed on 5+ directories but referral traffic is negligible
**Cause**: Most startup directories have modest traffic (5K-25K/mo). Individual referrals may be single-digit daily visitors. The value is cumulative backlinks and long-tail discovery, not immediate traffic spikes.
**Solution**: Shift expectations — directory submissions are an SEO play (backlinks compound over months) and social proof play (listed on X directories), not a traffic acquisition strategy. For traffic, pair with Reddit, Hacker News Show HN, and Product Hunt. Use `/sales-audience-growth` for high-traffic acquisition channels.

### Not sure which directories to prioritize
**Symptom**: Overwhelmed by the number of options, limited time
**Cause**: There are 50+ startup directories. Submitting to all of them is time-consuming and most have diminishing returns.
**Solution**: Start with the 7 directories in the first-party skills list — they cover the range of backlink strength and models. Submit to instant-listing platforms first (takes 10 minutes each), then queue the editorial ones. If you have time for more, check the third-party platforms table. Prioritize by backlink strength and relevance to your product category.

### Listings going live at random times, ruining coordinated launch
**Symptom**: Wanted a coordinated launch but editorial queues approved at unpredictable times
**Cause**: Free editorial queues (CtrlAlt.cc, MicroLaunch, LaunchingNext) don't guarantee timing. Your listing may go live weeks before or after you planned.
**Solution**: Only coordinate timing on platforms you control: instant-listing platforms (SoloPush, ProductBurst) and paid fast-track options. Treat free editorial submissions as "bonus" listings that will go live eventually. Don't plan your Product Hunt launch around when editorial queues approve you — those are independent.
