# Online Reputation Management Strategy Guide

## Review generation tactics

### SMS vs email for review requests

| Channel | Open rate | Response rate | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SMS** | 90%+ within 3 min | 10-20% | Service businesses, appointments, in-person interactions |
| **Email** | 20-30% | 5-15% | Ecommerce, B2B, longer consideration purchases |
| **In-person (tablet/kiosk)** | N/A | 20-40% | Retail, restaurants, healthcare waiting rooms |

**SMS wins for most local/service businesses.** Send within 1 hour of the interaction while the experience is fresh.

### Review request best practices

1. **Timing**: Send within 1 hour of service completion. Longer delays = lower response rates.
2. **Personalize**: Include the customer's name, the specific service, and the staff member who helped them.
3. **Direct link**: Link directly to the Google review form (not your Google Business Profile page). The fewer clicks, the higher the conversion.
4. **One platform per request**: Don't ask customers to review on Google AND Yelp AND Facebook. Pick one (usually Google).
5. **Follow up once**: A single reminder 2-3 days later is acceptable. More than that is pushy.
6. **Don't incentivize**: No discounts, freebies, or rewards for reviews. This violates Google's policies and can get all your reviews removed.
7. **Don't gate**: Send review requests to ALL customers, not just ones you think are happy. Selective solicitation (review gating) violates Google policy.

### Google review link format

```
https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID
```
Find your Place ID: https://developers.google.com/maps/documentation/places/web-service/place-id

### Review request template (SMS)

```
Hi {first_name}, thanks for visiting {business_name} today!
Would you take 30 seconds to share your experience?
{google_review_link}
Your feedback means a lot to {staff_name} and the team.
```

## Responding to reviews

### Response framework

**Positive reviews (4-5 stars):**
- Thank them by name
- Reference something specific they mentioned
- Keep it brief (2-3 sentences)
- Don't be overly promotional

**Negative reviews (1-3 stars):**
1. **Acknowledge**: "Thank you for sharing your experience, {name}."
2. **Empathize**: "I'm sorry to hear about {specific issue}."
3. **Take responsibility**: Don't blame the customer or make excuses.
4. **Take offline**: "I'd like to make this right — please contact me directly at {phone/email}."
5. **Follow up privately**: Call or email within 24 hours to resolve.
6. **Ask for update** (optional): After resolving, ask if they'd consider updating their review. Never pressure.

**Fake/spam reviews:**
1. Flag the review via Google Business Profile (Report review → select violation type)
2. Respond publicly: "We don't have any record of this visit. Please contact us directly so we can look into this."
3. Google removes ~10-15% of flagged reviews. If denied, escalate via GBP support.
4. Document the review for your records in case of a pattern.

### Response time targets

| Review type | Target response time |
|---|---|
| Negative (1-2 stars) | Within 24 hours |
| Neutral (3 stars) | Within 48 hours |
| Positive (4-5 stars) | Within 72 hours |

## Business listings management

### NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone)

NAP consistency across directories is a local search ranking factor. Inconsistencies confuse search engines and customers.

**Priority directories to fix first:**
1. Google Business Profile (most important)
2. Yelp
3. Facebook
4. Bing Places
5. Apple Maps
6. Industry-specific directories (Healthgrades, Avvo, TripAdvisor, etc.)

### Google Business Profile optimization

1. **Claim and verify** all locations
2. **Complete every field**: business name, address, phone, website, hours, categories, description, services, products
3. **Add photos**: exterior, interior, team, products/services (businesses with photos get 42% more direction requests)
4. **Choose correct primary category** (most important ranking signal for maps)
5. **Add secondary categories** for all relevant services
6. **Post regularly**: Google Posts (weekly) signal an active business
7. **Enable messaging** if you can respond quickly
8. **Respond to all reviews** (Google confirms review responses are a ranking factor)
9. **Add Q&A**: Pre-seed with common questions and answers

### Common listing issues

| Issue | Impact | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Different phone numbers across directories | Confuses search engines, splits authority | Audit and standardize to one number per location |
| Duplicate listings on same platform | Splits reviews, confuses customers | Merge or remove duplicates |
| Outdated hours | Frustrated customers, lower trust | Set up automated sync via ORM tool |
| Missing categories | Lower search visibility | Add all relevant categories on each platform |
| Inconsistent business name | Hurts local SEO | Use exact legal name consistently |

## Reputation crisis response

### Immediate actions (first 24 hours)

1. **Assess the scope**: Is this one angry review, a social media pile-on, or a news story?
2. **Don't delete or argue**: Public arguments and deleted responses go viral.
3. **Acknowledge publicly**: Post a brief, empathetic response acknowledging the issue.
4. **Take offline**: Provide a direct contact for resolution.
5. **Brief your team**: Ensure everyone knows the situation and the official response.

### Recovery plan (weeks 1-4)

1. **Fix the root cause**: If the complaint is valid, fix the operational issue.
2. **Increase review volume**: Ramp up review requests to all customers. Fresh positive reviews push negatives down.
3. **Respond to every review**: Show future customers you're engaged and responsive.
4. **Monitor daily**: Set up alerts for your business name + key complaint terms.
5. **Consider professional help**: For news stories or sustained attacks, a reputation management firm may be needed (different from ORM tools).

### What NOT to do during a crisis

- Don't offer free services publicly (looks like a bribe, others will expect it)
- Don't accuse the reviewer of lying (even if they are — handle privately)
- Don't have friends/family post fake positive reviews (Google detects same-IP patterns)
- Don't respond emotionally — draft, wait 1 hour, revise, then post

## Multi-location reputation management

### Key metrics per location

| Metric | Target | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Review volume (last 90 days) | 10+ per month | Fresh reviews rank higher, dilute negatives |
| Average rating | 4.0+ (ideally 4.5+) | Below 4.0 loses significant traffic |
| Response rate | 100% | Google confirms responses are a ranking factor |
| Response time | <24 hours for negatives | Shows active management |
| Listing accuracy | 100% NAP match | Foundation for local SEO |

### Benchmarking locations

- Rank locations by rating, volume, and response time
- Identify bottom performers — usually low volume (not low quality)
- Share best practices from top performers
- Set location-level targets with accountability

## Google review policy compliance

### What's allowed
- Asking all customers for reviews after a transaction
- Sending SMS or email review request links
- Displaying a QR code that links to your Google review page
- Responding to all reviews
- Reporting fake or spam reviews

### What's prohibited
- Offering incentives for reviews (discounts, free items, gift cards)
- Review gating (pre-screening to only send happy customers)
- Purchasing fake reviews
- Reviewing your own business
- Having employees review your business
- Posting reviews for locations customers didn't visit
- Discouraging negative reviews or asking for removal
