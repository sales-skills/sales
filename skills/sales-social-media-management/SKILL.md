#### When to choose each platform

- **Sprout Social** — best for data-driven teams that need deep analytics and CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot). Premium pricing ($199/seat) is justified when you need reporting depth and social listening in one platform. Overkill for solo creators or budget-conscious teams.
- **Hootsuite** — best for power users managing many networks with the most integrations (3x more than Sprout). OwlyWriter AI repurposes top-performing content automatically. Good middle ground between price and features.
- **Buffer** — best for solo creators and small teams that want clean, simple scheduling without a learning curve. Most affordable entry point ($6/mo per channel). No inbox management or advanced analytics.
- **Agorapulse** — best for agencies and mid-market teams that need approval workflows, social inbox, and solid reporting at a lower price than Sprout. Strongest Sprout alternative for teams that find Sprout too expensive.
- **Sendible** — best for agencies that need white-label reports and client management. Most of Sprout's features at ~10x lower price point. Scales well for multi-client operations.
- **Later** — best for visual brands focused on Instagram and TikTok. Grid preview for visual planning. Linkin.bio for driving traffic from Instagram. Limited inbox and analytics.
- **Planable** — best for teams where content approval is the bottleneck. Visual approval workflows are the core feature. No analytics or inbox — purely a content collaboration tool.
- **Statusbrew** — best for teams wanting Sprout-level features at 30-40% lower cost. Strong analytics and unified inbox. Good alternative when Sprout pricing is prohibitive.

### Setting up a publishing workflow

#### 1. Define your content mix

Start with a content ratio framework:

| Content type | % of posts | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Educational** | 30-40% | Teach, share insights, how-to content |
| **Engaging** | 20-30% | Questions, polls, user-generated content, conversations |
| **Promotional** | 10-20% | Product features, offers, launches |
| **Entertainment** | 10-20% | Memes, trending topics, behind-the-scenes |
| **Community** | 10% | Customer spotlights, employee content, testimonials |

#### 2. Build a content calendar

- **Weekly cadence**: Decide minimum posting frequency per network (e.g., 3-5/week on Instagram, 1/day on LinkedIn, 2-3/day on X)
- **Content themes**: Assign recurring themes to days (e.g., "Tip Tuesday", "Customer Friday")
- **Batch creation**: Create content in batches (weekly or bi-weekly) rather than daily — reduces burnout and improves consistency
- **Queue system**: Fill a content queue that auto-publishes at optimal times. Most tools support this.

#### 3. Optimize posting times

- **Start with defaults** — most tools suggest optimal times based on industry averages
- **Adjust with data** — after 2-4 weeks, review your analytics for actual engagement peaks per network
- **Network-specific patterns**:
  - Instagram: Weekday mornings (7-9 AM), lunch (11 AM-1 PM), evenings (7-9 PM)
  - LinkedIn: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM and 12-1 PM
  - X/Twitter: Weekdays 9 AM-3 PM, higher volume = more visibility
  - TikTok: Evenings and weekends, varies heavily by audience
- **Time zones matter** — schedule for your audience's time zone, not yours

### Engagement management

#### Inbox workflow

1. **Triage** — scan all incoming messages, tag by type (support, sales, praise, complaint, spam)
2. **Prioritize** — respond to complaints and support questions within 1 hour during business hours
3. **Route** — assign complex issues to the right team member
4. **Respond** — use saved replies for common questions, personalize for complaints
5. **Close** — mark resolved conversations as complete

#### Response time benchmarks

| Channel | Expected response time | Best practice |
|---|---|---|
| DMs | Under 1 hour | Critical for customer satisfaction |
| Comments | Under 4 hours | Drives engagement and algorithm visibility |
| Mentions | Under 24 hours | Builds community and reputation |
| Reviews | Under 48 hours | Impacts buying decisions for prospects |

### Team collaboration

- **Roles and permissions** — configure access levels (admin, editor, analyst, contributor)
- **Content approval** — multi-step review before publishing (at minimum: creator → reviewer → publish)
- **Internal notes** — add context to messages/tasks without public visibility
- **Collision detection** — prevent two team members from replying to the same message (Sprout, Hootsuite, Agorapulse support this)

### Analytics and ROI

#### Key metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Engagement rate** | Interactions ÷ impressions | Content resonance |
| **Reach** | Unique accounts that saw content | Audience size |
| **Impressions** | Total views (including repeat) | Content distribution |
| **Click-through rate** | Link clicks ÷ impressions | Traffic generation |
| **Audience growth rate** | Net new followers ÷ total | Community growth |
| **Response time** | Avg time to first reply | Customer experience |
| **Share of voice** | Your mentions ÷ total market | Competitive position |

#### Proving social ROI

- **UTM tracking** — tag every link with campaign/source/medium parameters
- **Attribution** — connect social clicks to website conversions in Google Analytics
- **Revenue attribution** — track social-driven revenue via Shopify, Salesforce, or HubSpot
- **Earned media value (EMV)** — estimate equivalent ad spend for organic reach

## Gotchas

*Best-effort from research — review these, especially items about pricing and features that may change.*

- **Per-seat pricing can explode.** Sprout Social ($199-399/seat), Hootsuite ($99/mo base), and enterprise tools charge per user. A 5-person team on Sprout Professional = $1,500/month. Budget for team size, not just features.
- **"Social listening" means different things.** Sprout and Hootsuite call it social listening. But their listening is social-focused — it doesn't cover news, broadcast, or print. For full media monitoring, you need Meltwater or Brandwatch.
- **Free tiers are very limited.** Buffer's free plan covers 3 channels with basic scheduling. Most other tools' free tiers are trials, not permanent. Don't choose a tool based on free tier capabilities.
- **Network API changes break features.** Instagram, X, and TikTok regularly change their APIs. Features that work today (scheduling Reels, auto-publishing Stories) may break tomorrow. Choose tools with a track record of fast API adaptation.
- **Analytics don't aggregate perfectly.** Each network defines "engagement" and "impressions" differently. Cross-network comparison charts in any tool are approximations, not exact comparisons. Focus on trends within networks, not absolute cross-network numbers.
- **Approval workflows slow publishing.** They're essential for brand safety but can kill timeliness. Set up fast-track paths for time-sensitive content (trending topics, breaking news) alongside standard approval flows.

## Troubleshooting

### Engagement dropping despite consistent posting
**Symptom**: Same posting frequency but impressions and engagement are declining
**Cause**: Algorithm changes, content fatigue, audience growth stalling, or posting at suboptimal times
**Solution**: Review content mix — increase engaging and educational content, reduce promotional. Test new content formats (Reels, carousels, polls). Check if posting times still align with audience activity. Experiment with posting frequency (sometimes less is more). Review competitor content for what's working in your space.

### Team members posting inconsistent content
**Symptom**: Brand voice varies across posts, off-brand content going live
**Cause**: No content guidelines, no approval workflow, too many people with publish access
**Solution**: Create a brand voice document with examples. Implement approval workflows (even a simple creator → reviewer → publish chain). Restrict publish access to trained team members. Use saved reply libraries for engagement responses.

### Can't justify the cost of a premium tool
**Symptom**: Team wants Sprout or Hootsuite but leadership won't approve the budget
**Cause**: Social ROI isn't visible to decision-makers, or the team hasn't quantified the time savings
**Solution**: Run a 30-day trial and document: hours saved per week, response time improvement, posts published vs. previous method. Calculate the hourly cost of manual work vs. tool cost. Present the comparison as cost-per-hour-saved, not just subscription price.
